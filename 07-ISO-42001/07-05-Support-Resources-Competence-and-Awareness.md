# Introduction

Even the most comprehensive AI governance framework cannot succeed without the necessary support. Leadership may establish clear objectives, and planning may identify appropriate controls, but organizations still require qualified personnel, sufficient resources, effective communication, and reliable documented information to operate the Artificial Intelligence Management System successfully.

Clause 7 of ISO/IEC 42001 focuses on these supporting elements. It ensures that organizations have the capabilities needed to implement, maintain, monitor, and continually improve AI governance throughout the AI lifecycle.

Support is more than providing technology or funding. It involves developing competent people, promoting awareness, facilitating communication, and maintaining reliable governance documentation.

---

# Purpose of Clause 7

Clause 7 ensures that organizations have the necessary support mechanisms to operate an effective Artificial Intelligence Management System.

The clause addresses:

- Resources.
- Competence.
- Awareness.
- Communication.
- Documented information.

These elements enable governance activities established in earlier clauses to function effectively.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Allocate sufficient human, technical, financial, and organizational resources to support the implementation and continual improvement of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.
- Identify competency requirements for all personnel involved in AI governance, including leadership, technical teams, risk management, compliance, cybersecurity, and internal audit.
- Develop competency through a combination of education, practical experience, professional certifications, mentoring, and ongoing training.
- Maintain documented evidence of competence, such as training records, certifications, skills assessments, and competency matrices.
- Review resource needs regularly to ensure governance capabilities remain aligned with business growth, AI adoption, and regulatory changes.
- Encourage cross-functional learning so that technical and non-technical teams develop a shared understanding of responsible AI governance.
- Integrate AI competency development into existing organizational learning and professional development programs.
- Treat competence as a continuous process by updating skills and knowledge as Artificial Intelligence technologies, standards, and regulations evolve.

These practices ensure that organizations have the people, capabilities, and supporting resources necessary to operate an effective and sustainable Artificial Intelligence Management System.

By investing in both organizational resources and employee competence, the company strengthens its Artificial Intelligence Management System and builds the expertise needed to manage AI responsibly as technologies and regulatory expectations continue to evolve.

# Awareness and Communication in the Artificial Intelligence Management System

Having competent personnel is only one part of effective AI governance. Every individual who interacts with Artificial Intelligence systems must also understand the organization's governance expectations, their responsibilities, and the potential consequences of inappropriate AI use.

ISO/IEC 42001 recognizes that awareness and communication are essential components of an effective Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). Employees who understand AI governance principles are more likely to make informed decisions, recognize potential risks, report issues promptly, and support the organization's commitment to responsible AI.

Clause 7 therefore requires organizations to establish processes for raising awareness and communicating relevant AI governance information to both internal and external stakeholders.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Develop AI awareness programs for all personnel whose work may influence or be affected by Artificial Intelligence, not only technical specialists.
- Tailor awareness content to the responsibilities of different audiences, including executives, managers, developers, business users, compliance teams, and third-party providers.
- Communicate the Artificial Intelligence Policy, governance objectives, reporting procedures, and ethical expectations through regular and accessible communication channels.

- Review and update awareness materials regularly to reflect changes in AI technologies, regulations, organizational policies, and lessons learned from operational experience.

These practices help build an informed workforce that understands its responsibilities and actively contributes to responsible, secure, and trustworthy Artificial Intelligence governance.

# Documented Information in the Artificial Intelligence Management System

An Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) relies on accurate, complete, and well-managed documented information. Policies, procedures, risk assessments, governance decisions, audit results, and operational records all provide evidence that the organization is managing Artificial Intelligence in a consistent and responsible manner.

ISO/IEC 42001 requires organizations to create, maintain, protect, and control documented information necessary for the effective operation of the Artificial Intelligence Management System. Documentation also provides objective evidence during internal audits, external certification audits, management reviews, and regulatory inspections.

Well-managed documentation promotes consistency, accountability, traceability, and continual improvement throughout the AI lifecycle.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Maintain documented information that is sufficient to support the effective operation, monitoring, and continual improvement of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.
- Clearly distinguish between governance documents that define requirements and records that provide evidence those requirements have been implemented.
- Establish document control processes covering creation, review, approval, version management, distribution, retention, and secure disposal.
- Protect sensitive AI governance documentation using appropriate access controls, encryption, information classification, and backup procedures.
- Review and update documented information regularly to reflect organizational changes, new AI technologies, regulatory developments, audit findings, and lessons learned.
- Define retention periods that satisfy legal, regulatory, contractual, and business requirements while preserving important governance evidence.
- Use centralized electronic document management systems where appropriate to improve consistency, traceability, and accessibility.
- Ensure documentation is readily available to authorized personnel and auditors while preventing unauthorized modification or disclosure.

These practices strengthen governance, improve operational consistency, support certification activities, and provide reliable evidence that the Artificial Intelligence Management System is functioning as intended.

# Practical Example

A multinational pharmaceutical company implements ISO/IEC 42001 to govern Artificial Intelligence used in clinical research, manufacturing quality control, supply chain forecasting, and regulatory reporting. When a new AI regulation introduces additional transparency requirements, the compliance team updates the AI governance procedures and risk assessment methodology. The revised documents are reviewed, approved, distributed through the organization's governance portal, and communicated to relevant personnel. During an external certification audit, the organization quickly demonstrates compliance by providing complete, current, and traceable documentation, reinforcing the effectiveness and maturity of its Artificial Intelligence Management System.
