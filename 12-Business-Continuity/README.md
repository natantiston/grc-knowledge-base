> *"Prepare for disruption. Protect critical services. Recover with confidence."*

# Overview

Business Continuity Management (BCM) is a critical discipline within Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) that enables organizations to prepare for, respond to, recover from, and continually improve after disruptive events. Modern organizations face a wide range of threats, including cyberattacks, natural disasters, system failures, pandemics, supply chain disruptions, and human error. A mature BCM program ensures that essential business services remain available while minimizing operational, financial, legal, and reputational impacts.

This chapter provides a comprehensive guide to Business Continuity Management, covering governance, Business Impact Analysis (BIA), risk assessment, continuity strategies, Disaster Recovery (DR), Crisis Management, Operational Resilience, Cyber Resilience, Cloud Continuity, international standards, documentation, performance measurement, and continual improvement.

Designed for cybersecurity professionals, GRC practitioners, risk managers, IT leaders, auditors, compliance officers, and business executives, this chapter combines internationally recognized best practices with practical implementation guidance aligned with modern resilience frameworks.

# Chapter Summary

Business Continuity Management is no longer limited to disaster recovery—it is a strategic capability that enables organizations to maintain critical business services under adverse conditions. By integrating governance, risk management, recovery planning, cybersecurity, operational resilience, and continual improvement, organizations can reduce disruption, protect stakeholders, and sustain business operations in an increasingly complex threat landscape.

This chapter provides a practical roadmap for designing, implementing, and continuously improving a Business Continuity Management System aligned with international standards and industry best practices. Whether supporting ISO 22301 certification, strengthening cyber resilience, or improving enterprise operational resilience, the concepts presented in this chapter form an essential foundation for every modern GRC professional.
