# Chapter 2 – Risk Management

Risk management is one of the core pillars of Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC). It provides organizations with a structured approach for identifying, assessing, treating, monitoring, and communicating risks that may affect the achievement of strategic and operational objectives.

This chapter introduces internationally recognized risk management concepts, principles, governance structures, and frameworks used across industries worldwide. You will learn how organizations make informed, risk-based decisions, establish effective governance, assign accountability, communicate risks to stakeholders, and build resilient enterprise risk management programs.

Whether you are pursuing a career in Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC), Information Security, Internal Audit, Enterprise Risk Management (ERM), or Cybersecurity, the knowledge in this chapter forms an essential foundation for understanding how organizations manage uncertainty while achieving business objectives.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

- Understand the principles and objectives of risk management.
- Explain the relationship between governance and risk management.
- Identify and classify organizational risks.
- Perform risk analysis and risk evaluation.
- Select appropriate risk treatment strategies.
- Explain the concept of risk acceptance.
- Understand risk monitoring, reporting, and continual improvement.
- Differentiate Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) from Integrated Risk Management (IRM).
- Explain risk ownership, accountability, and the Three Lines Model.
- Understand risk communication and stakeholder engagement.
- Compare internationally recognized risk management frameworks and standards.
