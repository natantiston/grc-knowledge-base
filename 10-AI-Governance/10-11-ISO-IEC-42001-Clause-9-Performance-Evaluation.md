# Introduction

Establishing governance controls and operational processes is only the beginning of an effective Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). Organizations must also determine whether these controls continue to operate effectively and whether AI systems consistently achieve their intended objectives. ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 9 focuses on **Performance Evaluation**, requiring organizations to monitor, measure, analyze, and evaluate both the performance of AI systems and the effectiveness of the governance processes supporting them.

Performance evaluation provides objective evidence that governance activities are functioning as intended. Through continuous monitoring and measurement, organizations can identify deviations, detect emerging risks, verify compliance with policies and regulations, evaluate operational performance, and support informed decision-making. This enables management to take timely corrective actions and continually improve the effectiveness of the AI Management System.

---

# Purpose of Clause 9

Clause 9 ensures that organizations evaluate the effectiveness of their Artificial Intelligence Management System.

Its objectives include:

- Measuring AI performance.
- Monitoring governance effectiveness.
- Evaluating operational controls.
- Identifying emerging risks.
- Supporting regulatory compliance.
- Providing evidence for decision-making.
- Driving continual improvement.
- Demonstrating governance maturity.

Performance evaluation confirms whether governance objectives are being achieved.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Establish measurable performance objectives for AI systems and governance processes.
- Define monitoring activities based on organizational risks and business objectives.
- Combine automated monitoring with manual governance reviews.
- Document monitoring methods, measurement criteria, and evaluation results.

- Use performance data to support risk management and management decision-making.

- Integrate monitoring results into the organization's continual improvement process.

# Practical Example

A global insurance company operates AI systems for claims processing, fraud detection, and customer service automation. To evaluate the effectiveness of its Artificial Intelligence Management System, the organization establishes a comprehensive monitoring program that tracks prediction accuracy, processing times, system availability, model drift, cybersecurity events, privacy incidents, and compliance with internal governance policies. Operational dashboards provide real-time visibility into key performance indicators, while automated alerts notify governance teams whenever predefined thresholds are exceeded.

Each month, governance specialists analyze monitoring reports to identify trends, evaluate control effectiveness, and determine whether AI systems continue to satisfy business and regulatory requirements. Significant findings are presented to executive management and used to update AI risk assessments, improve governance controls, and initiate corrective actions where necessary. By implementing structured monitoring and measurement processes aligned with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 9, the organization maintains confidence in the performance, reliability, and governance of its AI systems.

# Internal Audit

Internal audits are a fundamental component of an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 9 requires organizations to conduct internal audits at planned intervals to determine whether the AI Management System conforms to the organization's governance requirements, the requirements of ISO/IEC 42001, and whether it is effectively implemented and maintained. Internal audits provide independent assurance that governance processes are functioning as intended and identify opportunities for continual improvement.

An AI internal audit evaluates more than the technical performance of AI systems. It examines governance structures, leadership commitment, risk management, operational controls, documented information, regulatory compliance, and the effectiveness of processes established throughout the AI lifecycle. Audit results provide management with objective evidence for decision-making and help strengthen organizational trust in AI systems.

---

# Purpose of Internal Audits

Internal audits provide independent evaluation of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

The objectives include:

- Verifying conformity with ISO/IEC 42001.
- Evaluating governance effectiveness.
- Assessing operational controls.
- Confirming compliance with organizational policies.
- Identifying weaknesses and improvement opportunities.
- Verifying corrective actions.
- Supporting management reviews.
- Promoting continual improvement.

Internal audits strengthen confidence in the organization's AI governance program.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Establish a documented, risk-based AI internal audit program.
- Ensure auditors are independent of the activities being audited.
- Define clear audit objectives, scope, and criteria before each audit.
- Collect sufficient objective evidence to support audit conclusions.
- Classify findings consistently and communicate them promptly.
- Track corrective actions until implementation is verified.
- Use audit results to improve governance processes and operational controls.
- Integrate AI internal audits with broader Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) and Internal Audit programs where appropriate.

# Practical Example

A multinational healthcare organization performs an annual internal audit of its Artificial Intelligence Management System covering AI-powered diagnostic support, patient scheduling, and clinical decision-support applications. The audit team reviews governance policies, AI risk assessments, operational procedures, access controls, monitoring records, validation reports, incident logs, and compliance documentation. Interviews are conducted with AI developers, cybersecurity specialists, clinicians, and governance personnel to verify that operational practices align with ISO/IEC 42001 requirements.

During the audit, the team identifies a minor nonconformity related to incomplete documentation of model retraining activities and an opportunity to improve monitoring of third-party AI services. Corrective actions are assigned to the responsible departments with defined deadlines, and follow-up reviews confirm successful implementation. The audit results are presented during the management review meeting, enabling executive leadership to make informed decisions regarding governance improvements and future AI initiatives. By conducting structured internal audits, the organization strengthens governance effectiveness, regulatory readiness, and stakeholder confidence in its AI systems.

# Management Review

Management review is a critical requirement of ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 9 and serves as the mechanism through which top management evaluates the overall performance and effectiveness of the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). While monitoring activities provide operational data and internal audits verify conformity, management reviews ensure that executive leadership uses this information to make strategic decisions regarding the organization's AI governance program.

The purpose of a management review is not simply to receive reports, but to determine whether the AI Management System remains suitable, adequate, and effective in achieving organizational objectives. Through periodic reviews, top management evaluates governance performance, AI risks, operational effectiveness, regulatory developments, audit results, resource requirements, and opportunities for continual improvement. These reviews demonstrate leadership commitment and ensure that AI governance remains aligned with the organization's strategic direction.

---

# Purpose of Management Reviews

Management reviews enable executive leadership to evaluate the overall effectiveness of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

- Evaluating governance performance.
- Reviewing AI risks.
- Assessing achievement of AI objectives.

- Evaluating operational performance.
- Confirming regulatory compliance.
- Allocating resources.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.

# Practical Example

A global manufacturing company conducts quarterly AI Governance Committee meetings and an annual executive management review of its Artificial Intelligence Management System. During the annual review, senior leadership evaluates AI performance dashboards, internal audit results, AI risk assessments, regulatory updates, cybersecurity incidents, privacy metrics, and the status of corrective actions. The review identifies increasing reliance on third-party AI services and recommends additional supplier oversight, expanded employee training, and enhanced monitoring of high-risk AI applications.

Based on the review outcomes, executive management approves revisions to the AI governance policy, allocates additional funding for governance automation, updates organizational AI objectives, and assigns responsibility for implementing new controls. Progress on these actions is tracked through subsequent governance meetings and verified during the next internal audit cycle. By conducting structured management reviews aligned with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 9, the organization ensures that its Artificial Intelligence Management System remains effective, aligned with business objectives, and capable of adapting to evolving technologies and regulatory requirements.

# AI Performance Metrics

Measuring the performance of an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) requires organizations to establish meaningful metrics that evaluate both the effectiveness of AI systems and the governance processes supporting them. ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 9 encourages organizations to define performance indicators that provide objective evidence for monitoring, management reviews, internal audits, and continual improvement. These metrics help determine whether AI systems are achieving business objectives while operating in a secure, responsible, and compliant manner.

AI performance metrics should extend beyond technical accuracy. Organizations should also measure governance effectiveness, risk management, security, privacy, compliance, operational efficiency, and stakeholder confidence. By combining technical and governance metrics, organizations gain a comprehensive understanding of AI performance and can make informed decisions regarding improvements, resource allocation, and future AI initiatives.

---

# Purpose of AI Performance Metrics

Performance metrics help organizations evaluate whether AI systems and governance processes are operating effectively.

The objectives include:

- Measuring AI performance.
- Evaluating governance effectiveness.
- Monitoring AI-related risks.
- Supporting management decisions.
- Demonstrating regulatory compliance.
- Tracking business value.
- Identifying improvement opportunities.
- Supporting continual improvement.

Well-designed metrics provide objective evidence for effective AI governance.

# Practical Example

A multinational retail organization operates AI systems for product recommendations, inventory forecasting, and fraud detection. To evaluate the effectiveness of its Artificial Intelligence Management System, the organization establishes a balanced set of performance metrics covering technical performance, governance, security, compliance, and business outcomes. Technical dashboards monitor prediction accuracy, model drift, response times, and system availability, while governance dashboards track AI risk assessment completion, policy compliance, internal audit findings, and corrective action status. Executive reports also include privacy incidents, security events, supplier assessment completion, and customer satisfaction trends.

Each quarter, executive leadership reviews these metrics to evaluate whether AI systems continue to deliver business value while operating within the organization's defined risk appetite. Declining model accuracy or increasing security incidents automatically trigger investigations, while governance metrics help identify areas requiring additional resources or process improvements. By implementing a comprehensive AI performance measurement framework aligned with ISO/IEC 42001 Clause 9, the organization gains objective evidence to support decision-making, strengthen governance, and drive continual improvement across its AI portfolio.
