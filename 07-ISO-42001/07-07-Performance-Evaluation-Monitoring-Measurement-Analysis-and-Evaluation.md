# Introduction

Implementing governance controls is only the beginning of an effective Artificial Intelligence Management System. Organizations must also determine whether those controls are achieving their intended outcomes.

Clause 9 of ISO/IEC 42001 focuses on evaluating the performance of the AIMS through monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation. Rather than relying on assumptions, organizations use objective evidence to determine whether AI systems remain secure, trustworthy, compliant, and aligned with business objectives.

Performance evaluation enables leadership to identify strengths, detect weaknesses, prioritize improvements, and demonstrate governance effectiveness to auditors, regulators, customers, and other stakeholders.

---

# Purpose of Clause 9

Clause 9 ensures that organizations:

- Monitor AI governance performance.
- Measure AI system effectiveness.
- Analyze governance data.
- Evaluate governance objectives.
- Identify improvement opportunities.
- Support evidence-based decision-making.
- Demonstrate continual improvement.

Performance evaluation confirms whether governance activities deliver the expected results.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Establish structured monitoring and measurement processes that evaluate both AI system performance and governance effectiveness.
- Define meaningful KPIs and KRIs aligned with organizational objectives, risk appetite, regulatory obligations, and stakeholder expectations.
- Collect reliable data from multiple operational, technical, security, compliance, and business sources to support evidence-based evaluations.
- Analyze performance trends regularly to identify emerging risks, recurring issues, and opportunities for operational or governance improvements.
- Evaluate results against predefined objectives, performance targets, legal requirements, and internal governance standards.
- Use performance information to support executive decision-making, resource allocation, risk management, and continual improvement initiatives.
- Periodically review KPIs and KRIs to ensure they remain relevant as AI technologies, business priorities, and regulatory requirements evolve.
- Document performance evaluation results to support management reviews, internal audits, certification activities, and regulatory inspections.

These practices enable organizations to objectively measure the effectiveness of their Artificial Intelligence Management System while promoting transparency, accountability, and continuous governance improvement.

---

# Practical Example

A multinational logistics company uses Artificial Intelligence to optimize delivery routes, forecast demand, and automate warehouse operations. To evaluate the effectiveness of its Artificial Intelligence Management System, the organization establishes KPIs for model accuracy, delivery efficiency, AI system availability, employee training completion, and corrective action closure rates. It also defines KRIs to monitor cybersecurity incidents, fairness exceptions, model drift events, and regulatory compliance issues. Data is collected from operational dashboards, security monitoring tools, audit reports, customer feedback, and AI performance logs.

Each month, the AI Governance Committee reviews monitoring results, analyzes trends, and compares actual performance against governance objectives and risk tolerance levels. When a gradual decline in forecasting accuracy is identified, the committee investigates the root cause, approves model retraining, updates operational procedures, and monitors post-implementation results. By continuously monitoring, measuring, analyzing, and evaluating AI performance, the organization maintains an effective Artificial Intelligence Management System that supports responsible AI and continual improvement.

# Internal Audit of the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS)

Internal audits are one of the most important mechanisms for evaluating whether an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) is functioning effectively. While continuous monitoring measures day-to-day operational performance, internal audits provide an independent and systematic assessment of whether governance processes comply with organizational requirements, ISO/IEC 42001, applicable regulations, and internal policies.

Clause 9 requires organizations to conduct planned internal audits at appropriate intervals to determine whether the Artificial Intelligence Management System is effectively implemented, maintained, and continually improved.

An effective internal audit program provides leadership with confidence that AI governance controls are operating as intended while identifying opportunities to strengthen governance before external certification audits or regulatory inspections.

---

# Purpose of Internal Audits

Internal audits help organizations determine whether the Artificial Intelligence Management System:

- Conforms to ISO/IEC 42001 requirements.
- Meets internal governance policies.
- Supports business objectives.
- Operates effectively.
- Is properly maintained.
- Identifies improvement opportunities.
- Demonstrates continual improvement.

Internal audits provide objective evidence of governance effectiveness.

---

# Objectives of an AI Internal Audit

A well-planned audit should verify:

- AI governance processes are implemented.
- AI risks are appropriately managed.
- Operational controls are functioning.
- Documentation is accurate and current.
- Regulatory obligations are being met.
- AI objectives are progressing as planned.
- Corrective actions are effective.

Audits focus on both compliance and operational effectiveness.

---

# Audit Principles

Internal audits should follow recognized auditing principles.

### Integrity

Auditors should perform their work honestly, objectively, and professionally.

---

### Independence

Auditors should remain independent from the activities they are auditing.

For example, AI developers should not audit their own projects.

---

### Evidence-Based Approach

Audit conclusions should be supported by objective evidence rather than opinions or assumptions.

Examples of evidence include:

- Policies.
- Procedures.
- Risk assessments.
- Training records.
- System logs.
- Meeting minutes.
- Operational reports.

---

### Confidentiality

Auditors should protect confidential business information, sensitive AI documentation, and personal data obtained during audit activities.

---

# Risk-Based Audit Planning

Not every AI system requires the same audit frequency.

Organizations should prioritize audits based on factors such as:

- AI system criticality.
- Business impact.
- Regulatory obligations.
- Previous audit findings.
- AI risk level.
- Operational complexity.
- Significant organizational changes.

Higher-risk AI systems should generally receive more frequent and detailed audits.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Establish a documented, risk-based internal audit program that covers all relevant elements of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.
- Define clear audit objectives, scope, criteria, methods, schedules, and responsibilities before each audit begins.
- Assign competent and independent auditors who possess appropriate knowledge of AI governance, ISO/IEC 42001, risk management, cybersecurity, privacy, and auditing techniques.
- Collect objective evidence through interviews, document reviews, observations, and technical verification rather than relying on assumptions or informal opinions.
- Prioritize audits for high-risk AI systems, critical business processes, and areas with previous findings or significant organizational changes.
- Maintain complete audit records, including plans, working papers, evidence, findings, reports, and follow-up activities, to demonstrate governance effectiveness and audit traceability.
- Ensure audit findings are communicated to relevant management promptly and that corrective actions are assigned, monitored, and verified for effectiveness.
- Regularly review and improve the internal audit program to reflect evolving AI technologies, regulatory requirements, organizational risks, and lessons learned.

These practices help organizations maintain an objective and effective internal audit function that supports continual improvement and strengthens confidence in the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

---

# Practical Example

A multinational financial institution implements a risk-based internal audit program as part of its ISO/IEC 42001 Artificial Intelligence Management System. The annual audit schedule prioritizes AI systems used for credit scoring, fraud detection, anti-money laundering, and customer identity verification because of their significant regulatory and business impact. Internal auditors define the audit scope, establish criteria based on ISO/IEC 42001, organizational AI policies, and financial regulations, and gather evidence through interviews with business owners, reviews of AI risk assessments, examination of model validation reports, observation of governance committee meetings, and analysis of monitoring dashboards and security logs.

During the audit, the team identifies that one fraud detection model has not completed its scheduled fairness reassessment after a major retraining exercise. Although no operational issues have occurred, the auditors record a nonconformity because organizational governance procedures require fairness validation after significant model changes. Management assigns corrective actions, updates the validation process, retrains relevant personnel, and verifies implementation during a follow-up audit. The findings strengthen governance maturity and demonstrate the organization's commitment to continual improvement and responsible Artificial Intelligence.

# Management Review of the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS)

Management review is the highest-level evaluation activity within an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). While monitoring provides operational information and internal audits assess conformity, management review enables top management to determine whether the AIMS remains suitable, adequate, effective, and aligned with the organization's strategic objectives.

ISO/IEC 42001 requires top management to periodically review the performance of the Artificial Intelligence Management System using objective evidence from across the organization. These reviews support strategic decision-making, resource allocation, risk management, and continual improvement.

Management review is not simply a compliance exercise—it is an opportunity for leadership to evaluate whether AI governance continues to support business value while managing AI-related risks responsibly.

---

# Purpose of Management Review

Management review enables top management to:

- Evaluate AIMS performance.
- Review governance objectives.
- Assess organizational risks.
- Verify regulatory compliance.
- Review resource adequacy.
- Evaluate opportunities for improvement.
- Make strategic governance decisions.

Management reviews ensure that AI governance remains aligned with changing business needs and stakeholder expectations.

---

# Objectives of Management Review

A management review should determine whether the Artificial Intelligence Management System:

- Continues to achieve its intended outcomes.
- Supports organizational strategy.
- Effectively manages AI risks.
- Complies with legal and regulatory requirements.
- Has sufficient resources.
- Remains suitable for future organizational needs.

The review provides leadership with confidence that governance activities remain effective.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Conduct management reviews at planned intervals using structured agendas, documented inputs, and clearly defined governance objectives.
- Ensure top management actively participates in reviewing AI governance performance, organizational risks, regulatory developments, resource needs, and continual improvement opportunities.
- Use objective evidence from monitoring activities, performance indicators, internal audits, risk assessments, AI incidents, customer feedback, and compliance evaluations to support decision-making.
- Evaluate whether the Artificial Intelligence Management System remains suitable, adequate, effective, and aligned with organizational strategy, business objectives, and stakeholder expectations.
- Record management review decisions, assigned responsibilities, implementation timelines, and follow-up activities to ensure accountability and traceability.
- Review resource adequacy regularly to determine whether additional personnel, funding, technology, training, or external expertise are required.
- Monitor completion and effectiveness of improvement actions arising from previous management reviews to verify that intended outcomes have been achieved.
- Integrate management reviews with broader enterprise governance processes, including information security, risk management, quality management, privacy, and business continuity where appropriate.

These practices enable executive leadership to maintain strategic oversight of the Artificial Intelligence Management System while ensuring governance remains responsive to organizational change, emerging risks, and evolving regulatory requirements.

---

# Practical Example

A multinational healthcare organization conducts quarterly management reviews as part of its ISO/IEC 42001 Artificial Intelligence Management System. Executive leadership, the AI Governance Committee, cybersecurity, compliance, privacy, legal, and clinical representatives review AI performance dashboards, internal audit findings, risk assessments, incident reports, fairness monitoring results, regulatory updates, and progress against governance objectives. During one review, leadership observes an increase in model drift across several diagnostic AI systems following changes in clinical data sources, as well as new regulatory guidance introducing additional transparency requirements for high-risk AI applications.

Following the review, management approves funding for enhanced model monitoring tools, updates the AI governance policy, revises validation procedures, assigns additional training for clinical AI teams, and establishes new objectives for explainability and regulatory compliance. Responsibilities, target dates, and success measures are documented, and progress is tracked during subsequent management reviews. Through structured leadership oversight and evidence-based decision-making, the organization ensures that its Artificial Intelligence Management System remains effective, resilient, and aligned with both business strategy and responsible AI principles.

# Clause 9 Implementation, Performance Evaluation Documentation, and Practical Guidance

# Common Performance Evaluation Challenges

Organizations frequently encounter challenges during Clause 9 implementation.

### Poor Quality Metrics

**Challenge**

Organizations collect excessive data but measure indicators that do not support governance objectives.

**Solution**

- Define meaningful KPIs and KRIs.
- Align metrics with business goals and AI risks.
- Periodically review measurement criteria.

---

### Incomplete Monitoring

**Challenge**

Monitoring focuses only on technical performance while overlooking governance effectiveness.

**Solution**

- Include security, privacy, fairness, compliance, human oversight, and operational performance indicators.
- Integrate monitoring across technical and business functions.

---

### Weak Internal Audits

**Challenge**

Audits emphasize documentation rather than operational effectiveness.

**Solution**

- Adopt a risk-based audit approach.
- Evaluate real operational practices.
- Verify implementation through objective evidence.

---

### Ineffective Management Reviews

**Challenge**

Leadership meetings become routine reporting sessions without strategic decision-making.

**Solution**

- Present trend analysis rather than isolated metrics.
- Focus on strategic risks and improvement opportunities.
- Assign measurable actions with defined owners and deadlines.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Establish structured performance evaluation processes that integrate monitoring, measurement, analysis, internal audits, and management reviews into a unified governance framework.
- Develop meaningful KPIs and KRIs that measure both AI system performance and the overall effectiveness of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.
- Use reliable operational data, audit evidence, incident reports, risk assessments, and stakeholder feedback to support objective decision-making.
- Conduct risk-based internal audits that evaluate governance implementation, operational effectiveness, regulatory compliance, and continual improvement opportunities.
- Ensure management reviews focus on strategic decision-making, organizational risks, resource adequacy, governance maturity, and long-term business objectives rather than operational reporting alone.
- Track corrective actions through formal registers, verify implementation, and assess whether actions effectively address identified root causes.
- Maintain complete documentation of monitoring activities, audit reports, management review outputs, and improvement initiatives to demonstrate governance effectiveness during certification and regulatory inspections.
- Regularly review and refine performance evaluation processes to address emerging AI technologies, evolving regulations, organizational changes, and lessons learned from operational experience.

These practices enable organizations to objectively assess the performance of their Artificial Intelligence Management System and provide the evidence necessary to support continual improvement, executive decision-making, and responsible AI governance.

# Practical Example

A multinational insurance company operates several Artificial Intelligence systems that support underwriting, fraud detection, claims processing, and customer service. As part of its ISO/IEC 42001 implementation, the organization establishes a comprehensive performance evaluation framework with executive dashboards, governance KPIs, risk indicators, quarterly internal audits, and semi-annual management reviews. Operational data is collected from AI monitoring platforms, cybersecurity systems, compliance assessments, customer feedback, incident reports, and internal audit findings. The AI Governance Committee reviews performance trends to determine whether governance objectives, regulatory obligations, and business targets continue to be achieved.

During one quarterly review, leadership identifies an increase in fairness-related exceptions within an automated underwriting model and recurring delays in closing corrective actions from previous audits. A root cause analysis reveals that recent model updates were not consistently following established validation procedures. Management approves process improvements, strengthens governance checkpoints, updates operational documentation, and assigns additional training for AI development teams. Follow-up monitoring confirms improved validation compliance, reduced fairness exceptions, and faster corrective action closure rates. By implementing a structured Clause 9 performance evaluation process, the organization demonstrates that its Artificial Intelligence Management System remains effective, evidence-based, and capable of supporting continual improvement.
