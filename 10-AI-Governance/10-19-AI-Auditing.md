# Introduction

As organizations increasingly rely on Artificial Intelligence to support business operations and decision-making, they must ensure that AI systems are governed effectively and operate in accordance with organizational policies, regulatory requirements, and recognized standards. Internal audits provide independent assurance that governance processes are functioning as intended and that AI risks are being managed appropriately.

Unlike routine operational reviews, an AI internal audit evaluates the effectiveness of the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). Auditors assess governance processes, operational controls, risk management practices, documentation, and compliance activities to determine whether the organization is meeting its AI governance objectives.

Within ISO/IEC 42001, internal audits are an essential mechanism for verifying conformity, identifying improvement opportunities, and supporting continual improvement.

---

# What is an AI Internal Audit?

An AI internal audit is an independent, objective, and systematic evaluation of an organization's Artificial Intelligence Management System and AI governance processes.

The audit determines whether:

- AI governance policies are implemented.
- AI risks are properly managed.
- Operational controls are effective.
- AI systems comply with organizational requirements.
- Regulatory obligations are addressed.
- Governance activities support continual improvement.

Internal audits provide assurance to management before external certification or regulatory assessments.

---

# Purpose of AI Internal Audits

AI internal audits help organizations evaluate both governance effectiveness and operational performance.

The objectives include:

- Verifying compliance with internal policies.
- Evaluating conformity with ISO/IEC 42001.
- Assessing governance effectiveness.
- Reviewing AI risk management processes.
- Identifying control weaknesses.
- Confirming operational consistency.
- Supporting continual improvement.
- Preparing for certification audits.

Internal audits provide management with confidence that the AI Management System is operating effectively.

# Common Challenges

Organizations may encounter several challenges when performing AI internal audits.

| Challenge | Description |
|------|------|
| Limited AI Expertise | Auditors may lack sufficient AI governance knowledge |
| Incomplete Documentation | Missing records reduce audit effectiveness |
| Poor Evidence Management | Evidence is difficult to locate or verify |

| Inconsistent Audit Scope | Different audits evaluate different governance areas |

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Develop a risk-based AI internal audit program.
- Define clear audit objectives and scope.
- Maintain auditor independence and objectivity.
- Use standardized audit checklists and procedures.
- Collect sufficient objective evidence.
- Interview both technical and business stakeholders.
- Document findings accurately and clearly.
- Track corrective actions through to completion.
- Report significant findings to senior management.
- Use audit results to improve the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

---

# Practical Example

A multinational insurance company implements an Artificial Intelligence Management System to govern AI models used for underwriting, fraud detection, and customer service automation. Before pursuing ISO/IEC 42001 certification, the organization conducts an internal audit covering governance policies, AI risk assessments, model validation, security controls, incident management, monitoring activities, and documented information.

The audit team interviews AI developers, governance personnel, compliance officers, and business managers while reviewing evidence from the AI register, risk assessments, model testing reports, deployment approvals, and management reviews. The audit identifies several improvement opportunities, including inconsistent documentation for model validation and incomplete monitoring records for one AI application.

Management assigns corrective actions, updates governance procedures, and verifies the effectiveness of the improvements before the certification audit. The internal audit strengthens governance maturity, improves operational consistency, and increases confidence that the Artificial Intelligence Management System conforms to ISO/IEC 42001 requirements.

Evidence enables auditors to verify that governance activities have been implemented effectively and consistently.

Within ISO/IEC 42001, documented information and objective evidence are essential for demonstrating compliance, supporting certification audits, and maintaining stakeholder confidence.

- Governance processes are implemented.
- AI risks are managed appropriately.
- Operational controls are functioning.

- Regulatory obligations are addressed.
- Continual improvement activities are taking place.

# Introduction

Achieving certification demonstrates that an organization has established a structured and effective approach to governing AI responsibly throughout its lifecycle.

Preparing for certification requires more than documenting policies and procedures. Organizations must demonstrate that governance processes are implemented, operational controls are effective, risks are managed, documented information is maintained, and continual improvement activities are actively performed.

Certification provides independent assurance that an organization's AI governance framework aligns with internationally recognized best practices, helping build trust with customers, regulators, business partners, investors, and other stakeholders.

---

# What is ISO/IEC 42001 Certification?

ISO/IEC 42001 certification is an independent assessment performed by an accredited certification body to determine whether an organization's Artificial Intelligence Management System conforms to the requirements of ISO/IEC 42001.

Certification demonstrates that the organization has established effective processes for:

- AI governance.
- AI risk management.
- AI lifecycle management.
- Operational controls.
- Performance evaluation.
- Continual improvement.
- Responsible AI practices.
- Documented information management.

Certification focuses on the effectiveness of the management system rather than the performance of a single AI model.

- Demonstrating commitment to responsible AI.
