# Introduction

Organizations are deploying Artificial Intelligence across an increasing number of business processes, products, and services. AI systems may include machine learning models, generative AI applications, intelligent automation, predictive analytics, computer vision, natural language processing, and decision-support systems.

As the number of AI systems grows, organizations must know what AI assets they own, where they are deployed, who is responsible for them, what data they process, and what risks they introduce. Without this visibility, effective governance becomes extremely difficult.

An AI Asset Inventory provides a centralized record of all AI-related assets within the organization. It enables management to identify AI systems, assign ownership, monitor risk, support compliance, and maintain operational oversight throughout the AI lifecycle.

Within an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS), maintaining an accurate AI Asset Inventory is a foundational governance activity that supports risk management, security, compliance, auditing, and continual improvement.

---

# What is an AI Asset Inventory?

An AI Asset Inventory is a structured and continuously maintained register of all AI-related assets used, developed, acquired, or managed by an organization.

The inventory provides visibility into AI assets throughout their lifecycle.

An AI asset may include:

- AI applications.
- Machine learning models.
- Large Language Models (LLMs).
- Training datasets.
- AI algorithms.
- AI services.
- AI APIs.
- AI platforms.
- AI development environments.
- AI infrastructure.

The inventory acts as the organization's authoritative source for AI governance.

---

# Why an AI Asset Inventory Matters

Organizations cannot govern what they cannot identify.

Maintaining an AI Asset Inventory helps organizations:

- Identify all AI systems in operation.
- Assign ownership and accountability.
- Support AI risk assessments.
- Improve regulatory compliance.
- Monitor AI lifecycle status.
- Manage AI changes.
- Support internal audits.
- Improve operational visibility.

An accurate inventory is essential for effective AI governance.

# Common Challenges

Organizations may encounter several challenges when maintaining an AI Asset Inventory.

| Challenge | Description |
|------|------|
| Shadow AI | Employees use AI tools without organizational approval |
| Rapid AI Adoption | New AI systems are introduced faster than governance processes |
| Decentralized Development | Different business units maintain separate AI assets |
| Third-Party AI Services | Limited visibility into externally hosted AI systems |
| Incomplete Documentation | Missing ownership or lifecycle information |
| Outdated Inventory | Inventory records are not updated after system changes |

Regular reviews and governance processes help maintain inventory accuracy.

---

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Maintain a centralized AI Asset Inventory.
- Record every AI system used across the organization.
- Assign both business and technical owners.
- Classify AI assets according to risk and criticality.
- Link AI assets to AI risk assessments.
- Review the inventory periodically.
- Update records whenever AI systems change.
- Include third-party AI services in the inventory.
- Integrate the inventory with AI governance and compliance activities.
- Use the inventory to support audits and management reviews.

---

# Practical Example

A global retail company uses AI for customer recommendations, inventory forecasting, fraud detection, and demand planning. As AI adoption grows across different departments, management realizes that there is no centralized record of AI systems, making it difficult to understand ownership, monitor risks, or demonstrate regulatory compliance.

The organization establishes a centralized AI Asset Inventory as part of its Artificial Intelligence Management System. Every AI system is assigned a unique identifier, business owner, technical owner, lifecycle status, risk classification, deployment location, and review schedule. Third-party AI services used by marketing and customer support teams are also included.

During an internal audit, the inventory helps auditors quickly identify all AI systems, verify governance documentation, and confirm that high-risk AI applications have completed the required risk assessments. The centralized inventory improves governance visibility, strengthens accountability, and supports effective AI lifecycle management across the enterprise.

- A well-maintained AI Asset Inventory is a foundational component of a mature Artificial Intelligence Management System.

# Introduction

As organizations adopt Artificial Intelligence across multiple business functions, governance requires more than simply knowing which AI systems exist. Management also needs detailed governance information about each AI system, including its purpose, ownership, risk level, regulatory status, approvals, lifecycle stage, and ongoing monitoring activities.

An AI Register provides this governance-focused information. It serves as a centralized record of AI systems and their associated governance details, enabling organizations to manage AI responsibly throughout the entire lifecycle.

While an AI Asset Inventory identifies and tracks AI assets, an AI Register provides the governance context surrounding those assets. Together, these two records provide comprehensive visibility into an organization's AI landscape.

Within ISO/IEC 42001, maintaining an AI Register supports accountability, documented information, operational control, risk management, internal audits, and continual improvement.

---

# What is an AI Register?

An AI Register is a centralized governance record that documents important information about every AI system used, developed, acquired, or managed by an organization.

The register helps organizations answer questions such as:

- Why does this AI system exist?
- Who owns it?
- What business process does it support?
- What risks have been identified?
- What approvals have been completed?
- What data does it process?
- What regulations apply?
- When should it be reviewed?

The AI Register becomes a key governance document for leadership, auditors, regulators, and operational teams.

---

# AI Register vs AI Asset Inventory

Although closely related, an AI Register and an AI Asset Inventory serve different purposes.

| AI Asset Inventory | AI Register |
|------|------|
| Focuses on identifying AI assets | Focuses on governing AI systems |
| Lists AI assets owned or used | Records governance information for each AI system |
| Supports operational visibility | Supports governance and compliance |
| Tracks lifecycle status | Tracks ownership, approvals, risks, and compliance |
| Supports asset management | Supports enterprise AI governance |

Most organizations maintain both records as complementary governance tools.
