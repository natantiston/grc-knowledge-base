# Introduction

Understanding the principles of AI governance is essential, but applying those principles in real organizational environments is what transforms knowledge into capability. Every organization adopting Artificial Intelligence must make governance decisions involving leadership, business strategy, cybersecurity, legal compliance, privacy, ethics, risk management, and operational performance. ISO/IEC 42001 provides a structured framework that enables organizations to coordinate these activities within a single Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS), ensuring that AI systems remain trustworthy, secure, transparent, and aligned with business objectives.

This lesson presents a comprehensive case study that demonstrates how an organization can implement AI governance in practice. While the example is fictional, it reflects governance challenges commonly encountered across industries such as finance, healthcare, telecommunications, manufacturing, and technology. The case study illustrates how governance structures, risk management, compliance activities, operational controls, internal audits, and continual improvement work together to support responsible AI throughout the entire AI lifecycle.

# Practical Example

GlobalTech begins its AI governance transformation by establishing an AI Governance Committee composed of representatives from executive leadership, cybersecurity, legal, privacy, risk management, data science, business operations, and internal audit. The committee develops enterprise-wide AI governance policies, defines governance roles, introduces standardized AI risk assessments, and creates centralized documentation for AI systems. AI governance is fully integrated into the organization's existing ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Management System and Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platform, ensuring that governance activities become part of normal business operations rather than isolated compliance exercises.

During the first year of implementation, the organization performs AI risk assessments for every major AI initiative, deploys continuous monitoring dashboards, strengthens third-party AI oversight, and conducts its first internal audit against ISO/IEC 42001 requirements. Audit findings identify several opportunities to improve documentation consistency and model validation procedures, leading to corrective actions and updated governance processes. Executive management reviews governance performance quarterly, ensuring that AI governance remains aligned with business strategy while supporting responsible innovation and long-term organizational resilience.

# AI Governance Implementation Roadmap

Successfully implementing an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) requires a structured and phased approach. Organizations should avoid attempting to implement every governance process simultaneously. Instead, AI governance should be introduced progressively by establishing leadership commitment, defining governance structures, assessing risks, implementing controls, monitoring performance, and continually improving governance maturity. A phased implementation reduces organizational disruption while allowing governance processes to mature over time.

ISO/IEC 42001 follows the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) model, enabling organizations to build governance capabilities through continual improvement. Each phase builds upon the previous one, ensuring that AI governance becomes embedded within existing business processes, enterprise risk management, information security, and corporate governance activities.

# Practical Example

Following approval of its AI governance strategy, GlobalTech Solutions develops a two-year implementation roadmap aligned with ISO/IEC 42001. During the first phase, executive management establishes governance objectives, appoints an AI Governance Committee, defines governance roles, and allocates implementation resources. The second phase focuses on creating enterprise AI policies, building an inventory of AI systems, conducting risk assessments, and integrating governance processes with the organization's existing Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) platform and ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Management System.

As implementation progresses, governance controls are introduced across AI development, deployment, monitoring, and third-party management processes. Employees receive role-based AI governance training, while governance dashboards provide real-time visibility into AI risks, compliance activities, and operational performance. Internal audits validate implementation progress, management reviews evaluate governance maturity, and corrective actions are implemented to address identified gaps. By following a structured implementation roadmap, GlobalTech successfully establishes a sustainable Artificial Intelligence Management System that supports responsible AI, regulatory readiness, and long-term business resilience.
