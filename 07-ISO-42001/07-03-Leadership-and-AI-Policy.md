# Introduction

No management system can succeed without active leadership. Policies, procedures, and technical controls provide structure, but it is leadership that establishes direction, allocates resources, defines accountability, and promotes a culture that supports responsible Artificial Intelligence.

Clause 5 of ISO/IEC 42001 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment to the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). Leadership is responsible for ensuring that AI governance is aligned with organizational objectives, integrated into business processes, supported with adequate resources, and continually improved.

AI governance is not solely an information technology responsibility. Decisions about AI often involve ethics, legal obligations, privacy, cybersecurity, business strategy, and risk management. As a result, executive leadership plays a central role in ensuring AI is developed and used responsibly across the organization.

---

# Purpose of Clause 5

Clause 5 establishes leadership responsibilities for the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

It focuses on:

- Leadership commitment.
- AI governance.
- Artificial Intelligence Policy.
- Organizational roles.
- Responsibilities.
- Accountability.
- Organizational culture.

These elements ensure that AI governance is embedded throughout the organization rather than operating as an isolated technical initiative.

---

# Leadership Commitment

Top management must actively demonstrate commitment to the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

Leadership should:

- Support responsible AI adoption.
- Promote ethical decision-making.
- Allocate appropriate resources.
- Define governance priorities.
- Monitor AI performance.
- Encourage continual improvement.
- Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory obligations.

Visible leadership commitment is critical to building trust in AI governance across the organization.

---

# Integrating AI Governance into Business Processes

AI governance should not operate independently from business operations.

Leadership should integrate AI governance into:

- Corporate governance.
- Enterprise risk management.
- Information security.
- Privacy management.
- Business continuity.
- Procurement.
- Software development.
- Human resources.
- Strategic planning.

Integration reduces duplication and ensures AI governance supports organizational objectives.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Ensure executive leadership actively sponsors and participates in AI governance rather than delegating responsibility solely to technical teams.
- Integrate AI governance into corporate governance, enterprise risk management, information security, privacy, and business continuity programs.
- Establish governance structures that clearly define decision-making authority, accountability, and oversight responsibilities for AI initiatives.
- Allocate sufficient financial, technical, and human resources to support the implementation and continual improvement of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.
- Promote an organizational culture that values responsible innovation, transparency, ethical decision-making, and continuous learning.
- Regularly review AI governance performance through management reporting, audits, risk assessments, and key performance indicators.
- Encourage cross-functional collaboration among business leaders, legal advisors, compliance professionals, cybersecurity teams, data scientists, and operational managers.
- Demonstrate leadership commitment through visible participation in governance activities, policy approvals, strategic planning, and continual improvement initiatives.

These practices help establish a governance culture in which Artificial Intelligence is managed responsibly, aligned with business objectives, and supported by executive leadership at every stage of the AI lifecycle.

---

# Practical Example

A global financial services organization launches an enterprise-wide Artificial Intelligence program to improve fraud detection, customer support, investment analysis, and operational efficiency. Rather than assigning AI governance solely to the information technology department, executive leadership establishes an AI Governance Committee chaired by the Chief Risk Officer and supported by representatives from legal, compliance, cybersecurity, privacy, data science, and business operations. The committee is responsible for approving AI policies, reviewing high-risk AI initiatives, monitoring regulatory developments, and overseeing continual improvement of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.

Senior leadership also integrates AI governance into existing enterprise governance processes by including AI-related risks in board-level risk reporting, allocating dedicated funding for AI governance activities, and requiring management reviews of AI performance. Through visible leadership commitment, the organization builds a governance culture that encourages innovation while ensuring that Artificial Intelligence systems remain trustworthy, transparent, secure, and aligned with strategic business objectives.

# Artificial Intelligence Policy and Leadership Accountability

Leadership commitment is demonstrated not only through words but also through documented direction and clear accountability. One of the most important responsibilities of top management under ISO/IEC 42001 is establishing an **Artificial Intelligence Policy** that communicates the organization's commitment to responsible AI governance.

The AI Policy provides the strategic direction for the Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). It defines the organization's governance principles, establishes expectations for employees and stakeholders, and serves as the foundation for AI objectives, operational controls, and continual improvement.

---

# What is an Artificial Intelligence Policy?

An Artificial Intelligence Policy is a high-level management document approved by top management that establishes the organization's intentions and direction regarding the responsible development, acquisition, deployment, operation, and monitoring of AI systems.

The policy communicates:

- Leadership commitment.
- AI governance principles.
- Organizational objectives.
- Risk management expectations.
- Ethical commitments.
- Compliance obligations.
- Continual improvement objectives.

It applies to all personnel involved in AI activities throughout the organization.

---

# Purpose of the AI Policy

The Artificial Intelligence Policy helps organizations:

- Establish governance expectations.
- Promote responsible AI use.
- Support strategic objectives.
- Define organizational commitment.
- Communicate leadership priorities.
- Align AI governance with business goals.
- Provide direction for operational procedures.

The policy acts as the foundation upon which the Artificial Intelligence Management System is built.

---

# Leadership Accountability

ISO/IEC 42001 emphasizes accountability rather than simply assigning responsibilities.

Leadership remains accountable for the effectiveness of the Artificial Intelligence Management System even when operational tasks are delegated.

Examples of leadership accountability include:

- Approving AI governance policies.
- Monitoring AI governance performance.
- Reviewing AI risks.
- Providing adequate resources.
- Supporting regulatory compliance.
- Encouraging ethical AI practices.

Delegation of activities does not transfer accountability.

# Reviewing the AI Policy

Reviews may be triggered by:

- Regulatory changes.
- New AI technologies.
- Organizational restructuring.
- Significant AI incidents.
- Audit findings.
- Business strategy changes.

Regular reviews ensure the policy continues to support organizational objectives and evolving AI governance requirements.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Develop an Artificial Intelligence Policy that reflects the organization's strategic objectives, governance principles, and commitment to responsible AI.
- Ensure the policy is formally approved by top management and communicated throughout the organization.
- Define clear governance principles covering fairness, transparency, accountability, human oversight, privacy, security, and continual improvement.
- Clearly distinguish between responsibility for performing AI governance activities and leadership accountability for the effectiveness of the Artificial Intelligence Management System.
- Establish governance committees where appropriate to support executive decision-making while maintaining overall accountability at the leadership level.
- Review the AI Policy regularly to address changes in regulations, technology, organizational strategy, and lessons learned from AI operations.
- Integrate the AI Policy with existing corporate governance, information security, privacy, and enterprise risk management policies to create a consistent governance framework.
- Reinforce the policy through awareness programs, leadership communications, and regular employee training to promote a culture of responsible AI.

These practices help ensure that the Artificial Intelligence Policy remains a living governance document that guides decision-making, supports compliance, and fosters trust in the organization's use of Artificial Intelligence.

---

# Practical Example

A multinational healthcare provider establishes an Artificial Intelligence Policy as part of its ISO/IEC 42001 implementation. The policy is approved by the Executive Board and outlines the organization's commitment to patient safety, transparency, privacy protection, human oversight, regulatory compliance, and ethical AI. It applies to all AI systems used in clinical decision support, patient engagement, administrative automation, and medical research. The policy also defines the responsibilities of executive leadership, the AI Governance Committee, clinical departments, information technology, cybersecurity, legal, and compliance teams.

To ensure the policy is effective, the organization integrates it into employee onboarding, annual training, and governance workshops. Following the introduction of new healthcare AI regulations, executive leadership conducts a formal policy review, updates governance requirements for high-risk AI systems, and strengthens approval procedures for externally acquired AI services. This continual review process helps maintain alignment between the organization's AI governance framework, regulatory obligations, and strategic healthcare objectives.
