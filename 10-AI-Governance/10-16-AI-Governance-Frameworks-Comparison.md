# Introduction

As Artificial Intelligence becomes increasingly integrated into business operations, organizations require structured governance frameworks to ensure AI systems are secure, trustworthy, ethical, and compliant. Multiple AI governance frameworks have emerged to help organizations manage AI-related risks and establish responsible governance practices.

Two of the most influential frameworks are **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** and the **NIST Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)**.

Although both frameworks promote trustworthy AI, they were developed for different purposes and use different approaches. ISO/IEC 42001 is an international management system standard that defines requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). In contrast, the NIST AI RMF is a voluntary risk management framework that provides guidance for identifying, assessing, and managing AI-related risks.

Understanding how these frameworks complement one another enables organizations to build stronger AI governance programs while improving operational resilience and regulatory readiness.

---

# What is ISO/IEC 42001?

ISO/IEC 42001 is the world's first international management system standard specifically designed for Artificial Intelligence.

Its primary objective is to establish an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) that enables organizations to:

- Govern AI responsibly.
- Manage AI risks.
- Establish organizational accountability.
- Integrate AI governance into business operations.
- Support continual improvement.
- Demonstrate effective AI governance through certification.

ISO/IEC 42001 follows the Annex SL structure used by other ISO management system standards, making it easier to integrate with standards such as ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO 9001.

---

# What is the NIST AI Risk Management Framework?

The NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) is a voluntary framework developed to help organizations identify, assess, manage, and monitor risks associated with AI systems.

Rather than defining certification requirements, the framework provides practical guidance for implementing trustworthy AI practices.

The framework emphasizes:

- AI risk management.
- Trustworthy AI.
- Governance.
- Human oversight.
- Continuous monitoring.
- Risk-informed decision-making.

The NIST AI RMF can be adopted by organizations of all sizes and across all industries.

---

| ISO/IEC 42001 | NIST AI RMF |

| Establish an AI Management System | Manage AI risks |
| Define management system requirements | Provide practical risk management guidance |
| Support certification | Support voluntary implementation |
| Continual improvement | Trustworthy AI outcomes |

Organizations often benefit from implementing both frameworks together.

# Introduction

As Artificial Intelligence continues to transform industries, organizations must address not only technical and operational challenges but also increasing regulatory expectations. Two of the most significant developments in AI governance are **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** and the **European Union AI Act**.

Although both promote responsible and trustworthy AI, they serve different purposes.

ISO/IEC 42001 is an international management system standard that provides a structured framework for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS). Organizations may seek independent certification to demonstrate conformity with the standard.

The EU AI Act, on the other hand, is legislation that establishes legally binding requirements for AI systems placed on the European Union market or used within the European Union. It adopts a risk-based approach, imposing stricter obligations on AI systems that present higher risks to individuals and society.

Organizations operating in or serving customers within the European Union should understand both ISO/IEC 42001 and the EU AI Act, as they complement one another in building responsible AI governance.

---

# What is ISO/IEC 42001?

ISO/IEC 42001 is an international standard that specifies the requirements for an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS).

Its objectives include:

- Establishing AI governance.
- Managing AI risks.
- Defining organizational responsibilities.
- Supporting continual improvement.
- Promoting trustworthy AI.
- Enabling independent certification.

The standard is applicable to organizations of all sizes and industries that develop, provide, or use AI systems.

---

# What is the EU AI Act?

The EU AI Act is a regulatory framework established by the European Union to ensure that AI systems are safe, transparent, and respectful of fundamental rights.

The regulation introduces legal obligations based on the level of risk presented by AI systems.

The EU AI Act seeks to:

- Protect individuals and society.
- Promote trustworthy AI.
- Encourage responsible innovation.
- Harmonize AI rules across EU Member States.
- Reduce risks associated with high-risk AI systems.

Unlike ISO/IEC 42001, compliance with the EU AI Act is mandatory where the regulation applies.

Organizations often use ISO/IEC 42001 to strengthen governance while meeting regulatory expectations.

# Introduction

Artificial Intelligence systems depend on secure information, reliable infrastructure, effective governance, and responsible risk management. As organizations increasingly adopt AI technologies, they must manage not only AI-specific risks but also traditional information security risks such as unauthorized access, data breaches, cyberattacks, and system failures.

Two important international standards help organizations achieve these objectives: **ISO/IEC 42001** and **ISO/IEC 27001**.

Although both standards follow the ISO management system approach and share a similar structure, they focus on different areas. ISO/IEC 42001 establishes an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS) to govern the responsible development, deployment, and operation of AI systems. ISO/IEC 27001 establishes an Information Security Management System (ISMS) that protects the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets.

Organizations using AI often benefit from implementing both standards together because secure AI requires both effective AI governance and strong information security.

---

# What is ISO/IEC 42001?

ISO/IEC 42001 specifies the requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS).

- Governing AI responsibly.

- Establishing organizational accountability.
- Supporting trustworthy AI.
- Promoting continual improvement.
- Demonstrating effective AI governance through certification.

The standard applies to organizations that develop, provide, or use AI systems.

---

# What is ISO/IEC 27001?

ISO/IEC 27001 specifies the requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Information Security Management System (ISMS).

Its objectives include:

- Protecting information assets.
- Managing information security risks.
- Preventing unauthorized access.
- Maintaining confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
- Supporting regulatory compliance.
- Improving information security continuously.

The standard can be implemented by organizations of any size and industry.

# Practical Example

A multinational telecommunications company deploys AI across customer service, network operations, cybersecurity, fraud detection, and predictive maintenance. Initially, each department manages its AI systems independently, resulting in inconsistent governance practices, duplicated documentation, and varying approaches to risk management.

To improve consistency, executive management establishes an enterprise AI governance program based on ISO/IEC 42001. The organization creates an AI Governance Committee, aligns AI policies with its existing Information Security Management System (ISO/IEC 27001), integrates AI risks into the enterprise risk management program, and standardizes AI documentation, internal audits, and management reviews across all business units.

By integrating AI governance into existing enterprise processes, the organization improves collaboration, strengthens regulatory compliance, enhances executive oversight, and ensures that AI systems are managed consistently throughout the enterprise. This integrated approach supports trustworthy AI while improving operational efficiency and governance maturity.
