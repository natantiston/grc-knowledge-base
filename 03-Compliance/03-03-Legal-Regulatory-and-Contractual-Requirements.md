# Introduction

Every organization operates within an environment governed by numerous external and internal requirements. These requirements define how the organization conducts its business, protects its stakeholders, safeguards information, and fulfills its legal and contractual responsibilities.

Some requirements are established by governments through laws and regulations. Others arise from contracts, industry standards, customer expectations, or the organization's own internal policies and procedures.

An effective Compliance Management System (CMS) begins with identifying all applicable compliance obligations. Organizations cannot comply with requirements that they have not identified or understood.

For this reason, one of the first responsibilities of compliance professionals is to determine **what requirements apply to the organization**, **where they originate**, and **how they should be managed**.

---

# What Are Compliance Obligations?

**Compliance obligations** are the legal, regulatory, contractual, voluntary, and internal requirements that an organization must or chooses to comply with.

These obligations establish expectations for how the organization should operate, manage risks, protect stakeholders, and demonstrate responsible business practices.

Compliance obligations may be:

- Mandatory.
- Voluntary.
- Industry-specific.
- Country-specific.
- Customer-driven.
- Organization-specific.

Regardless of their source, organizations should identify, document, monitor, and periodically review all applicable obligations.

---

# Categories of Compliance Obligations

Compliance obligations generally fall into five major categories.

## Legal Requirements

These are obligations established by legislation enacted by national, regional, or local governments.

Examples include:

- Data protection laws.
- Labor and employment laws.
- Tax legislation.
- Environmental laws.
- Consumer protection laws.
- Anti-corruption laws.
- Intellectual property laws.

Legal requirements are mandatory and failure to comply may result in legal penalties, fines, civil liability, or criminal prosecution.

---

## Regulatory Requirements

Regulatory requirements are issued by government agencies or regulatory authorities responsible for overseeing specific industries or business activities.

Examples include:

- Financial services regulations.
- Telecommunications regulations.
- Healthcare regulations.
- Aviation regulations.
- Energy sector regulations.
- Critical infrastructure regulations.

Regulators often require organizations to:

- Submit reports.
- Undergo inspections.
- Maintain licenses.
- Demonstrate ongoing compliance.
- Notify significant incidents.

Failure to comply may result in enforcement actions, sanctions, suspension of operations, or revocation of operating licenses.

---

## Contractual Requirements

Organizations frequently agree to contractual obligations with customers, suppliers, business partners, and service providers.

These requirements become legally binding once contracts are signed.

Examples include:

- Service Level Agreements (SLAs).
- Confidentiality agreements.
- Data Processing Agreements (DPAs).
- Outsourcing contracts.
- Supplier agreements.
- Cloud service contracts.
- Security requirements in customer contracts.

Many cybersecurity and privacy obligations arise directly from contractual agreements rather than legislation.

Organizations should ensure that contractual commitments are incorporated into their compliance management processes.

# Why Identifying Compliance Obligations Is Important

Before an organization can develop policies, implement controls, or monitor compliance, it must first understand **which obligations apply** to its business.

Proper identification of compliance obligations helps organizations:

- Avoid regulatory violations.
- Meet contractual commitments.
- Protect customers and stakeholders.
- Reduce legal and financial risks.
- Improve governance.
- Support risk management.
- Prepare for audits and inspections.
- Demonstrate due diligence.
- Build trust with regulators, customers, and business partners.

Failure to identify applicable obligations can result in compliance gaps that expose the organization to unnecessary legal, operational, financial, and reputational risks.

A mature compliance program therefore begins with a comprehensive understanding of the organization's compliance landscape.
