# Introduction

Once an organization understands its business environment, assets, dependencies, and cybersecurity risks through the **Govern (GV)** and **Identify (ID)** Functions, the next step is to implement safeguards that reduce the likelihood and impact of cybersecurity incidents.

This is the purpose of the **Protect (PR)** Function.

The Protect Function focuses on implementing administrative, technical, and physical safeguards that secure information, systems, services, and business operations. While no organization can completely eliminate cyber risk, effective protective measures significantly reduce the opportunity for attackers to compromise critical assets.

The Protect Function emphasizes proactive risk reduction rather than reactive incident handling.

---

# Purpose of the Protect Function

The Protect Function helps organizations:

- Safeguard critical assets.
- Prevent unauthorized access.
- Reduce vulnerabilities.
- Protect sensitive information.
- Strengthen operational resilience.
- Support secure business operations.
- Minimize the likelihood of successful cyberattacks.
- Enable secure digital transformation.

Protection is an ongoing activity that evolves alongside technology, business processes, and the threat landscape.

---

# Why Protection Matters

Modern organizations face numerous cybersecurity threats, including:

- Ransomware.
- Phishing attacks.
- Credential theft.
- Insider threats.
- Supply chain attacks.
- Cloud misconfigurations.
- AI-related attacks.
- Exploitation of software vulnerabilities.

Without effective protective controls:

- Sensitive information may be exposed.
- Critical services may be disrupted.
- Financial losses may occur.
- Regulatory penalties may increase.
- Customer trust may be damaged.
- Business continuity may be compromised.

The Protect Function minimizes these risks by establishing layered defenses.

---

# Defense in Depth

The Protect Function is based on the principle of **Defense in Depth**.

Rather than relying on a single security control, organizations implement multiple complementary safeguards.

Example:

```
Security Awareness

↓

Identity Management

↓

Access Control

↓

Endpoint Protection

↓

Network Security

↓

Encryption

↓

Monitoring

↓

Incident Response
```

If one control fails, additional controls continue protecting the organization.

# Categories within the Protect Function

The Protect Function consists of several categories that work together to reduce cybersecurity risk.

| Category | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| PR.AA | Identity Management, Authentication, and Access Control |
| PR.AT | Awareness and Training |
| PR.DS | Data Security |
| PR.PS | Platform Security |
| PR.IR | Technology Infrastructure Resilience |

These categories address people, processes, technology, and information protection.

---

# Identity Management, Authentication, and Access Control (PR.AA)

Access to systems and information should be limited to authorized individuals.

Organizations should implement:

- Identity management.
- Authentication mechanisms.
- Authorization processes.
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC).
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Privileged Access Management (PAM).
- Least privilege.
- Account lifecycle management.

Effective access control significantly reduces unauthorized access.

---

# Awareness and Training (PR.AT)

Employees remain one of the most important cybersecurity defenses.

Organizations should provide:

- Security awareness programs.
- Phishing simulations.
- Role-based cybersecurity training.
- Executive awareness sessions.
- Secure remote work guidance.
- AI security awareness.

Well-informed employees help prevent many common cyber incidents.

---

# Data Security (PR.DS)

Data protection extends beyond preventing unauthorized access.

Organizations should implement:

- Data classification.
- Encryption.
- Secure backups.
- Data Loss Prevention (DLP).
- Secure data disposal.
- Data retention management.
- Integrity verification.

Protecting information throughout its lifecycle reduces confidentiality and integrity risks.

---

# Platform Security (PR.PS)

Organizations should secure technology platforms including:

- Servers.
- Workstations.
- Mobile devices.
- Cloud infrastructure.
- Containers.
- Virtual machines.
- Operational Technology (OT).
- Internet of Things (IoT).

Platform security typically includes:

- Secure configuration.
- Vulnerability management.
- Patch management.
- Malware protection.
- Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR).
- Configuration monitoring.

---

# Technology Infrastructure Resilience (PR.IR)

Infrastructure resilience ensures that technology continues supporting business operations during adverse conditions.

Examples include:

- Network redundancy.
- Backup power.
- High availability.
- Disaster recovery capabilities.
- Cloud resilience.
- Secure architecture.
- Network segmentation.

Resilient infrastructure minimizes operational disruption during cybersecurity incidents.

---

# Preventive vs Detective Controls

The Protect Function primarily implements **preventive controls**.

Examples:

Preventive:

- MFA.
- Encryption.
- Firewalls.
- Security awareness.
- Secure configuration.
- Access control.

Detective:

- Security monitoring.
- Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS).
- Security Information and Event Management (SIEM).

Although detection belongs primarily to the Detect Function, preventive and detective controls complement one another.

---

# Protection Supports Business Objectives

Cybersecurity protection should support business success.

Protective measures should enable:

- Digital transformation.
- Cloud adoption.
- Artificial Intelligence.
- Remote work.
- Customer trust.
- Regulatory compliance.
- Operational resilience.

Security should be integrated into business processes rather than acting as an obstacle to innovation.

# Best Practices

Organizations should:

- Implement multiple layers of preventive safeguards using the defense-in-depth principle to reduce the likelihood of successful cyberattacks.
- Protect identities, systems, applications, data, cloud services, operational technology, and AI environments through integrated administrative, technical, and physical controls.
- Apply the principles of least privilege, role-based access control, strong authentication, and privileged access management to minimize unauthorized access.
- Establish continuous security awareness and role-based training programs so employees understand their cybersecurity responsibilities and emerging threats.
- Protect sensitive information throughout its lifecycle using classification, encryption, secure storage, backup, retention, and secure disposal processes.
- Maintain secure technology platforms through standardized configurations, vulnerability management, patch management, endpoint protection, and configuration monitoring.
- Design resilient technology infrastructure that supports high availability, redundancy, disaster recovery, and secure business continuity.
- Align protective safeguards with business objectives, enterprise risk management, regulatory requirements, and organizational resilience goals to ensure cybersecurity enables rather than restricts business operations.

These practices help organizations establish a comprehensive protection strategy that reduces cyber risk, strengthens operational resilience, and supports long-term business success.

# Practical Example

A multinational financial services company operates online banking platforms, cloud-hosted applications, mobile banking services, AI-powered fraud detection systems, and international payment processing infrastructure. Following the completion of its asset inventory and cybersecurity risk assessment, the organization implements the Protect Function of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 by introducing multi-factor authentication for all employees, privileged access management for administrative accounts, encryption of customer data both in transit and at rest, and role-based access controls across business applications. Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR), secure configuration standards, automated patch management, and continuous vulnerability scanning are deployed to strengthen platform security, while regular phishing simulations and cybersecurity awareness training improve employee resilience against social engineering attacks.

To support operational resilience, the organization implements network segmentation, geographically redundant data centers, secure cloud architectures, and tested backup and disaster recovery procedures. Executive leadership monitors cybersecurity KPIs related to patch compliance, privileged access reviews, security awareness participation, and vulnerability remediation. By applying layered protective safeguards across people, processes, technology, and data, the organization significantly reduces cybersecurity risk while enabling secure digital banking services, regulatory compliance, and resilient business operations.
