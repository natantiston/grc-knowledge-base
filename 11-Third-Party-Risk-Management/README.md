# Chapter 11 – Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM)

## Overview

Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) is a critical discipline within Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) that enables organizations to identify, assess, monitor, and manage risks introduced by external vendors, suppliers, service providers, contractors, cloud providers, and business partners.

As organizations increasingly rely on outsourced services and digital supply chains, third-party relationships have become essential to business operations. However, these relationships also introduce cybersecurity, operational, legal, financial, privacy, and regulatory risks that must be effectively governed throughout the vendor lifecycle.

This chapter provides a comprehensive understanding of Third-Party Risk Management, covering governance, vendor identification, risk assessment, due diligence, information security assessments, contract management, regulatory compliance, continuous monitoring, incident management, reporting, and practical implementation through a real-world case study.

The content aligns with internationally recognized standards and best practices, including:

- ISO/IEC 27001
- ISO/IEC 27036
- ISO 31000
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0
- NIST SP 800-161 (Cyber Supply Chain Risk Management)
- GDPR
- SOC 2
- Industry best practices for Third-Party Risk Management

Whether you are a GRC professional, cybersecurity consultant, auditor, risk manager, compliance officer, or security leader, this chapter provides the knowledge required to establish and operate an effective Third-Party Risk Management program.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you will be able to:

- Explain the purpose and importance of Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM).
- Understand the complete vendor lifecycle from onboarding to offboarding.
- Establish governance structures for managing third-party risks.
- Perform vendor identification, classification, and criticality assessments.
- Conduct inherent and residual risk assessments.
- Perform vendor due diligence and financial assessments.
- Evaluate information security controls using security questionnaires, ISO 27001 certifications, and SOC reports.
- Understand contract risk management, security clauses, and service level agreements (SLAs).
- Apply privacy and regulatory compliance requirements to third-party relationships.
- Monitor vendors continuously and reassess risks throughout the relationship.
- Respond effectively to vendor-related cybersecurity incidents.
- Report vendor risks to management and support informed decision-making.
- Apply TPRM concepts through a practical enterprise case study.
